The Problem

E-commerce brand operators manage their business across 5-6 fragmented tools. Each shows a slice of reality. No tool connects inventory to ad spend to delivery speed to competitive pricing.

Marketing blind spot

Ads tools don't see inventory. Ads run on out-of-stock SKUs. Budget sits on underperformers. ROAS collapses before anyone notices.

Inventory blind spot

OMS tools don't see ad spend. When stock drops to 3 days, nobody throttles the campaigns burning £2,400/day on that SKU.

No cross-channel P&L

SKU-level contribution margin across Amazon, Flipkart, and Shopify? Most brands need a week of Excel. By then, data is stale.

Data Architecture

Four layers from raw ingestion to decision memory. Each layer adds proprietary value.

Layer A: Raw Store

Untouched API responses & CSVs. Audit trail. Re-derive anything from raw data.

Layer B: Canonical Schema

16 normalised PostgreSQL tables. Core: Unified SKU linking ASIN, FSN, Shopify Variant into one Terminal6 ID.

Layer C: Enriched / Computed

Cross-channel attribution, delivery speed scoring, inventory risk, competitive price indices. No third-party computes this.

Layer D: Decision Memory

Every agent decision: trigger → context → reasoning → policy check → outcome. This is the flywheel.

6 Throttle Signals

Every active SKU is scored on 6 signals. The composite drives all agent decisions.

1
Inventory Cover
Days of stock remaining. <3d = pause ads. 3-7d = throttle. >30d = push harder.
2
Margin Health
Current margin vs brand's floor. Below floor = stop spending. Above floor+5% = full aggression.
3
Catalogue Score
Listing quality: title, images, A+ content, video. Bad listing = bad CVR = wasted spend.
4
Rating & Reviews
<3.5 stars = pause. Negative spike = throttle + alert CX. >4.0 = boost eligible.
5
Price Competitiveness
Our price vs competitor. >15% more expensive = pause. <5% cheaper = push harder.
6
Delivery Speed
Same-day = 1.15x bid multiplier. 3D+ = 0.8x. Speed drives conversion.

Knowledge System: The Moat

Every diagnosis has two parts: the process (universal) and the interpretation (domain-specific). Terminal6 captures both as structured, composable layers.

Layer 1: Playbooks

Universal process. What factors to check, in what order. Category-agnostic. "Check if conversion dropped" but not "why" — that's the category card's job.

Layer 2: Category Cards

Domain expertise. How signals behave in a specific vertical. "Phone lifecycle drives accessories demand. Organic SEO lags new launches by 2-4 weeks."

Layer 3: Region Cards

Market context. India salary cycles, COD dynamics, festival calendars, logistics constraints. Affects all categories in a geography.

How Knowledge Composes at Runtime

Trigger: "D2C conversion dropped 15%" Playbook (Layer 1): Check funnel stage → ATC dropped, not sessions Check traffic source → organic flat, paid stable Check product concentration → top 3 SKUs affected Category Card (L2): "ATC dropped on specific SKUs" → check phone lifecycle Top SKUs entering decline phase (12-18 month cycle) Region Card (L3): Day 12 of month → salary cycle dip (historically lowest) No Amazon sale running → not cannibalisation Brand Context: Organic conv declining for 3 months (known trend) Sprig increased Meta spend (mix effect) Diagnosis: Salary cycle dip + phone lifecycle rotation + organic SEO lag. No action needed. Monitor 2 more days for recovery.

This diagnosis is impossible with a single-layer playbook. It requires composing universal process, category interpretation, regional context, and brand history.

Brand Directives: Living Strategy

Operator intent from meetings and ad-hoc decisions, captured as structured directives with priority stacking.

OVR
Override
Kill switch. Emergency stop. Halts all automated actions.
TAC
Tactical
"Don't touch THERMO-1L-BLK — PO landing tomorrow." Ad-hoc, expires in hours/days.
STR
Strategy
"Go aggressive on Bottles for 10 days, +30% bids, cap 50K/day." From weekly standup.
SEA
Seasonal
"Prime Day prep: 2x inventory by July 1." Calendar-driven, annual.
PRM
Permanent
"Margin floor 28% on Bottles." Set during onboarding, rarely changes.

Key principle: Directives give intent. The Policy Engine's hard constraints are never overridden. A "go aggressive" directive cannot push margin below the floor.

Context Assembly

Agents never see all 50K SKUs. The context assembler builds the right context per call — hierarchical, not monolithic.

Layer 1: Brand (~2K tokens)

Always present. Profile, policies, active directives, health summary.

Layer 2: Domain (~8K tokens)

Per agent. Growth sees ad data; Supply sees inventory. Filtered to relevant categories.

Layer 3: Task (~5K tokens)

Per decision. Deep dive on 5-10 SKUs: signals, P&L, ad history, competitor pricing, decision history.

Total: ~20K tokens per agent call. Focused context produces sharp decisions.

The Knowledge Flywheel

Every brand that onboards makes the system smarter for every other brand in the same category.

Phase 1 (1-3 brands)

Knowledge lives in brand folders. Patterns validated per-brand. High specificity, low generalisability.

Phase 2 (3-10 brands)

Cross-brand validation. Same pattern in 2+ brands graduates from brand learning to category card.

Phase 3 (10+ brands)

Category cards become authoritative. New brand inherits deep domain expertise from Day 1.

The Acceleration

Brand 1: Weeks of discovery. Brand 5: Days — category card already rich. Brand 20: Hours — agent is already an expert.
